Abstract
CCTV (Closed Circuit Television) cameras are commonly used for safety, security, deterring
illegal activities and for investigation purposes. Traditional systems require manual monitoring
of the large video volumes which often causes to miss crucial events due to human error. There
is a need to alleviate manual monitoring and move one step ahead to process the audio and
video streams in real time and detect anomalies to prevent mishaps and ensure public safety.
This project aims to develop an AI-powered anomaly detection system that combines computer
vision and audio analysis to automatically identify unusual activities and security threats in real
time. By integrating the intelligent detection with alerting tool, the system reduces high
dependence on continuous human supervision. The proposed work will take comprehensive
audio and video training datasets and will use model-based training to make a live anomaly
detection system that demonstrates how artificial intelligence can transform conventional
surveillance into a scalable and efficient solution for enhancing security in diverse
environments.
Introduction
In the modern world, CCTV cameras are installed almost everywhere from huge organizations
including educational institutes & workplaces to small shops, and from transport hubs to public
areas. They are widely used to maintain security, monitor activities, and play vital role in
investigations when unfortunate incidents occur. Although these systems are pretty useful, they
primarily operate in the conventional manner requiring humans to sit and watch live streams
or maybe complete footage after the incident has already occurred. This manual approach is
not only exhausting but also prone to human error, due to which many critical events could be
overlooked.
Empowering the surveillance systems with artificial intelligence can make such systems
smarter by detecting anomalous activities on their own. Instead of analyzing long video streams
to identify critical events, AI (Artificial Intelligence) models can analyze video and audio
streams in real time to identify potentially dangerous situations, strange behaviors or threats.
This presents an opportunity to shift from reactive monitoring to proactive monitoring where
the issues can be addressed as they arise.
To develop an AI-powered anomaly detection system for CCTV surveillance, the system
should integrate audio and video analysis to automatically identify unusual behaviors,
suspicious objects, loud or alarming sounds, even potential medical issues from human posture
and other anomalies. This integration would allow for real-time alerts and analytics to support
faster responses and better decision-making. By reducing the dependency on constant human
monitoring, the project aims to improve the reliability, efficiency, and effectiveness of
surveillance systems in real-world environments.
Goals and Objectives
The objectives of this project include:
- To design an AI-powered CCTV anomaly detection system.
- To identify critical audio anomalies such as screams, gunshots, or alarms.

- To build a real-time alert system for quick reporting of incidents.
- To adapt the system for different zones and varying environmental conditions.
- To provide additional analytics like heatmaps for understanding movement patterns.

Scope of the Project
A prototype system that combines audio & video analysis for anomaly detection will be
designed and developed as part of the project. The video analysis part will include features
such as posture and behavior recognition (including detection of medical issues like fainting),
suspicious object detection, overcrowding alerts, and monitoring of incidents such as
unauthorized entry. The audio module will detect unusual sounds like gunshots, screams, or
glass breaking. The system will also generate real-time alerts and provide basic analytics such
as heatmaps to analyze frequently used areas.
The focus will remain on real-time anomaly detection and alerting within the prototype.
Advanced features like forensic investigation, cloud-based large-scale deployment, or
integration with external security databases will not be included in the current scope. The final
system will demonstrate how AI can make CCTV surveillance more efficient, automated, and
useful in preventing or responding to security incidents.
Initial Study and Work Done so Far
The earliest work we reviewed was by Yu et al. [1]. They came up with a cloze-test based
method where the model predicted missing parts of a video and compared them with the actual
sequence. If the prediction was very different, the event was flagged as an anomaly. This idea
was interesting because it captured both spatial and temporal features effectively.
In 2022, Zaheer et al. [2] presented a Generative Cooperative Learning (GCL) framework that
worked in an unsupervised way. They used a generator and a discriminator in a cooperative
manner, making it possible to detect anomalies without labeled data. Zhao, M. et al. [3] also
worked in the same year, but their focus was on spatial-temporal correlations, mainly looking
at how objects and behaviors change across time and space. These works showed improvement
in accuracy and efficiency.
Also previously, Zhu, Chen, and Sultani [4] worked on smart surveillance in 2020. Their
method detected and localized abnormal events in both space and time, which made it quite
useful for CCTV style applications. Guerra, B. et al. [5] looked at another angle by working on
automatic pose recognition for risky situations, especially in assisted living. Their system
tracked skeletons to detect things like falls or abnormal postures, which was a different but
useful approach.
Later, Leporowski et al. [6] included audio along with video. They built an audio-visual dataset
for traffic surveillance and showed that combining the two signals gave better results, like
detecting a crash from both horn and visuals. Sun, L. et al. [7] then developed a high-speed

From this literature, we can see that although the field has moved forward, most works still
stayed narrow in scope. Some focused only on video, some on posture, some just in traffic, etc.
None of them really tried to combine multiple important factors like video, audio, posture, and
contextual awareness together. Our project aims to fill this gap by combining video and audio
anomaly detection, supported by posture analysis, zone sensitivity, and real-time alerts to make
surveillance more practical for Pakistani environments like hospitals, schools, and markets.
